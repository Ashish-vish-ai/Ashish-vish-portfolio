DemocracyOS

DemocracyOS is a civic technology platform that transforms representative SMS feedback into AI-synthesized government insights. By meeting citizens where they already communicate — on their phones — and processing that data with modern language models, we give policymakers a real-time pulse on public opinion without the bottleneck of traditional consultation.

Origin

Democracy is slow. Not because people don't care — but because the tools for participation were designed for a different century. I started DemocracyOS after observing that governments routinely made decisions with almost no structured feedback from the communities they represented. The problem wasn't a lack of willingness on either side. It was infrastructure.

Problem Solved

Traditional consultation is expensive, slow, and skewed toward citizens who are already engaged. Town halls exclude people who work night shifts. Online surveys get ignored. Social media is noise. DemocracyOS targets representativeness — gathering a statistically meaningful signal from a cross-section of the public, not just the loudest voices.

How It Works

Citizens receive short SMS prompts about active policy decisions. They respond in natural language. An AI layer reads, clusters, and synthesizes those responses into structured insight reports — highlighting themes, outliers, and sentiment shifts — and delivers them to government dashboard users in near-real time.

My Role

As Product Designer and Founder, I owned the full product vision — from citizen-facing SMS flows to the government analytics dashboard. I led UX research, interaction design, AI prompt architecture, and stakeholder presentations to local government partners.

Team

Small and focused: myself as product designer and founder, one back-end engineer, one AI/ML specialist, and an advisor with deep experience in local government procurement.

Timeline

Initial concept and research: Q3 2024. Prototype and first government pilot: Q4 2024. Refined product and second pilot cohort: Q1–Q2 2025.

Outcome

Two successful pilots with local government bodies. Strong early engagement metrics and positive qualitative feedback from both citizens and policymakers. Currently in active conversations with three additional government partners.

Discovery

Research

I conducted 14 in-depth interviews with city councilors, town planners, and community advocates across three municipalities. The pattern was consistent: everyone wanted better public input; no one had a practical way to get it at scale.

"We make decisions that affect thousands of people based on twelve voices in a Tuesday-night meeting."

— City Councilor, Pilot City A

"If it's not on my phone, it doesn't exist. I'm not going to a website to fill out a form."

— Citizen Research Participant

"We have spreadsheets of emails that no one has read. We don't lack data. We lack synthesis."

— Policy Analyst, Pilot City B

"Trust is the product. If people think their input vanishes into a void, they stop participating."

— Civic Tech Advisor

Synthesis

Four core design pillars emerged from the research to guide every product decision:

01

Collection

Meet citizens in existing channels. Zero new app installs. SMS-first, always.

02

Representation

Statistical sampling over convenience sampling. Amplify underrepresented voices by design.

03

Synthesis

AI doesn't replace human judgment — it makes the signal visible so humans can decide.

04

Trust & Empowerment

Citizens see their input acknowledged. Governments act on evidence. The loop closes.

Design

Guiding Principle

The hardest design constraint was invisibility. For citizens, the product needed to feel like a text from a neighbour, not government bureaucracy. For officials, it needed the authority of a briefing document, not another dashboard they'd ignore after day one.

Public Experience

The citizen-facing flow lives entirely in SMS. A short question. A prompt to reply. An acknowledgement that closes the loop.

DemocracyOS

Hi — your council is deciding on new cycling infrastructure for Oak Street. In a sentence or two, what matters most to you about this change? Reply any time.

I'd love a protected lane. My kids cycle to school and the current road feels dangerous, especially near the intersection at Maple.

DemocracyOS

Thank you — your input has been recorded and will inform the council's decision. Results are published at city.gov/oak-street.

Government Experience

The dashboard distills hundreds of responses into a briefing-ready summary — themes, sentiment, representative quotes, and a confidence score for each insight.

Impact

Both pilot governments reported that DemocracyOS gave them access to a quality and breadth of public input they had never achieved through traditional consultation. The 69% response rate — compared to the 3% industry average for civic surveys — suggests that channel friction, not civic apathy, was the barrier all along. Officials described the synthesized insight reports as "immediately usable" in briefings. Citizens in follow-up interviews expressed surprise that their responses felt acknowledged rather than discarded.

The project is ongoing. We're refining the AI synthesis layer for greater nuance, adding multilingual support, and building toward a sustainable SaaS model for municipal governments.
