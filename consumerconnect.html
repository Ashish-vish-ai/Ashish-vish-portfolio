Consumer Connect

An internal tool built for the sales team at CapitalXB to streamline client onboarding and document collection. It replaces fragmented email-based workflows with a structured portal that allows sales to request, track, and manage client information — all in one place.

What I Did

Owned design end-to-end — from mapping the existing email workflow to delivering screens for every stage of the onboarding process.

Workflow Design

Designed the end-to-end onboarding flow — from initial client creation to document collection, link dispatch, and follow-up query management.

User Flow Simplification

Translated a scattered, email-driven process into a step-by-step system that sales teams could follow without ambiguity or repetition.

UI Design

Designed all major screens: client creation, requirement selection, the customer-facing submission form, and the query and follow-up panel.

The Problem

The sales team at CapitalXB was running client onboarding entirely through email — a process that created friction at every step.

01

Fragmented communication

Sales relied on back-and-forth emails to collect documents, making the process unstructured and easy to lose track of.

02

No tracking visibility

There was no way to know what had been requested, what was received, and what was still pending for each client.

03

Repetitive manual work

Sales repeatedly asked for the same documents and clarifications client-by-client, causing delays and inconsistency.

Key Decisions

The specific calls I made — and why I made them.

01
Workflow Design

Centralized all onboarding steps into a single structured flow

The existing process was split across email threads, attachments, and mental notes. Rather than digitising individual pieces, I designed a single flow where every action — data input, document requests, and follow-ups — happens within one interface. This removed the context-switching that was making the process hard to track and inconsistent across clients.

02
Input Design

Dynamic requirement selection instead of a fixed document list

Different clients require different documents — a fixed form would either overwhelm everyone or miss critical inputs for specific cases. Letting sales dynamically select requirements made the system flexible without sacrificing structure. It also shifted responsibility to the person with context (the sales rep), rather than trying to encode every edge case into a rigid form.

03
Interaction Design

Link-based client interaction over direct back-and-forth

Instead of continuing ad-hoc email conversations, I designed a link-dispatch model where clients receive a structured submission form. This ensured responses arrived in a consistent, trackable format rather than scattered attachments. It also gave sales a clear view of what had been submitted and what was still outstanding — something email conversations fundamentally cannot provide.

Outcome

The project delivered a centralized onboarding system that replaced a messy, email-driven workflow with a structured and trackable process. Sales teams could manage client data, document collection, and follow-ups from a single portal — removing the fragmentation that was causing delays and inconsistency.

Personally, this project strengthened how I think about designing for operational efficiency. Internal tools are unforgiving — users aren't choosing to use them, and they have no patience for friction. Designing something that genuinely reduces manual effort, rather than just looking cleaner, requires understanding the workflow deeply before touching the interface. That shift in thinking — workflow first, UI second — is something I carry into every project now.
